A novated lease is one of the most popular salary packaging options in Australia — and for good reason. By running a car through your employer using pre-tax dollars, you can save thousands each year in income tax. Here's how it actually works.

## What Is a Novated Lease?

A novated lease is a three-way agreement between:

1. **You** — the employee
2. **Your employer** — who takes on the lease obligations
3. **A finance or fleet company** — who owns the vehicle

Your employer deducts your lease payments and agreed running costs from your **pre-tax salary** each pay period. Because the money comes out before income tax is calculated, your taxable income drops — and so does your tax bill.

If you leave the employer, the lease "denovates" back to you personally, and you make repayments directly to the finance company.

## What's Included in a Novated Lease Bundle?

A fully maintained novated lease typically bundles:

- Vehicle purchase price (financed)
- Registration and CTP insurance
- Comprehensive car insurance
- Fuel (or charging costs for EVs)
- Servicing and tyres
- Roadside assistance

All of these are paid from your pre-tax salary, making the effective cost significantly lower than purchasing and running the car from after-tax pay.

## The Fringe Benefits Tax (FBT) Factor

The ATO treats the private use of an employer-provided vehicle as a **fringe benefit**, which is normally subject to FBT at 47%. This would wipe out much of the tax advantage.

However, the FBT cost is incorporated into the lease structure and is typically paid for from a mix of pre-tax and **post-tax** employee contributions. A portion of each fortnightly payment comes from after-tax pay (the "employee contribution") to offset the FBT liability — reducing or eliminating the employer's FBT exposure.

The FBT is calculated on a **statutory formula**: the car's purchase price multiplied by a statutory rate (currently a flat **20%**), reduced by any employee contributions.

**FBT calculation (simplified):**
- Taxable value = Vehicle cost × 20% × (days available ÷ 365)
- Less: post-tax employee contributions
- FBT payable = Remaining taxable value × 2.0647 × 47%

The novated lease provider handles this calculation and structures your pre/post-tax split to manage the FBT liability efficiently.

## Real-Money Example

**Employee on $120,000 salary, leasing a $50,000 car:**

| Cost | Out-of-pocket purchase | Novated lease |
|---|---|---|
| Tax saving on $20,000 pre-tax payments | $0 | ~$6,000 (at 30% marginal rate) |
| GST on purchase price | $4,545 (can't claim) | $0 (employer claims input credit) |
| GST on running costs | ~$400/yr (can't claim) | ~$0 (claimed by employer) |
| **Estimated annual saving** | — | **$5,000–$8,000** |

The actual saving depends on the vehicle cost, usage, marginal tax rate, and running costs. Most novated lease providers offer detailed quotes showing the pre/post-tax split and net benefit.

## Electric Vehicles — The FBT Exemption

From 1 April 2025, **eligible electric vehicles** packaged through a novated lease are completely **exempt from FBT**. This means the entire lease payment (for a qualifying EV) can come from pre-tax salary with no post-tax contribution required to offset FBT.

To qualify, the vehicle must be:
- A **battery electric vehicle (BEV)** or a **plug-in hybrid** (PHEV — grandfathered until 1 April 2025 and certain subsequent arrangements)
- **Below the luxury car tax threshold** ($89,332 in 2025–26 for low-emission vehicles)
- **First held and used** on or after 1 July 2022

This makes EVs exceptionally attractive to package. On a $70,000 electric vehicle, the annual tax saving at a 30% marginal rate can exceed $8,000–$10,000 compared to buying the same car with after-tax pay.

## Things to Consider

**Does your employer offer novated leasing?**
Not all employers do. Most large corporate, government, and healthcare employers have arrangements with fleet management companies; many small businesses don't.

**Residual value**
At the end of the lease term (typically 3–5 years) you can pay the residual to own the car, re-lease, or return it. Getting the residual amount wrong can leave you with a gap.

**HECS repayment income**
The reportable fringe benefit from a novated lease may be included in your HELP repayment income, potentially triggering a higher HECS repayment rate even though your taxable income is lower.

**Net effect on salary**
Your gross salary is unchanged, but your take-home after tax will be lower than someone on the same salary without a novated lease (because taxable income is lower but you're also making post-tax contributions). The vehicle and running costs replace what would have been out-of-pocket spending.

## Is It Worth It?

The higher your marginal tax rate and the more you drive, the more valuable a novated lease becomes. At 30% or above with a medium-priced vehicle, the savings are typically substantial.
