Salary packaging — also called salary sacrifice — is one of the most effective legal strategies for reducing your income tax in Australia. Instead of paying for certain benefits out of your after-tax pay, you agree to receive a lower gross salary in exchange for non-cash benefits. Because those benefits come from your pre-tax income, your taxable income drops and so does your tax bill.

## How Salary Packaging Works

The mechanics are straightforward:

1. You agree with your employer to sacrifice part of your gross salary
2. Your employer provides an equivalent benefit (e.g. a laptop, car, or super contribution)
3. Your taxable income is reduced by the sacrificed amount
4. You pay less income tax

**Simple example:**

| | Without packaging | With packaging |
|---|---|---|
| Gross salary | $90,000 | $90,000 |
| Salary sacrificed (laptop) | $0 | $3,000 |
| Taxable income | $90,000 | $87,000 |
| Income tax saving | — | ~$900 |
| You effectively paid | $3,000 (after tax) | ~$2,100 (pre-tax) |

The saving comes from the difference between the pre-tax cost and what you'd have spent after tax.

## What Can Be Salary Packaged?

The ATO divides packaged items into two categories based on how they're taxed.

### Items With Fringe Benefits Tax (FBT)

Most benefits are subject to **Fringe Benefits Tax (FBT)**, which your employer pays at 47% on the grossed-up value. This often makes packaging these items not worthwhile unless your employer absorbs the FBT cost. Common FBT-liable items include:

- Cars (via novated lease — see below)
- Gym memberships
- Private health insurance

### FBT-Exempt Items

These are the sweet spot of salary packaging — benefits that carry **no FBT**, making them genuinely tax-reducing:

- **Superannuation contributions** — the most universally available option (see below)
- **Portable electronic devices** (one per year — laptop, phone, tablet)
- **Work-related software**
- **Work tools** used predominantly for employment purposes

### FBT-Exempt Employers (The Big Advantage)

Employees of certain organisations receive a concessional FBT treatment:

- **Public hospitals and public benevolent institutions (PBIs):** Can package up to **$9,010** in general living expenses per year FBT-free (mortgage, rent, utilities, groceries)
- **Other charities and not-for-profits:** Similar exemption up to $15,900 for PBIs that aren't hospitals
- **Ambulance services, some disability services:** Similar concessions

If you work for a hospital or charity, salary packaging living expenses is one of the most valuable tax strategies available to any Australian worker.

## Superannuation Salary Sacrifice

This is the most accessible packaging option for all employees. Instead of contributing from after-tax pay, you direct part of your gross salary into super before income tax is applied.

- Contributions are taxed at **15%** inside the fund (concessional rate)
- For most workers this is lower than their marginal income tax rate (30%, 37%, or 45%)
- The saving per dollar contributed = (marginal rate − 15%)

**Example — $100,000 salary, sacrifice $5,000 to super:**

| | Without sacrifice | With sacrifice |
|---|---|---|
| Taxable income | $100,000 | $95,000 |
| Income tax saving (30% bracket) | — | $1,500 |
| Contributions tax inside fund | — | $750 |
| **Net saving** | — | **$750** |

The concessional contributions cap is **$30,000 per year** (including your employer's SGC contributions). Exceeding the cap triggers excess contributions tax.

## Novated Leasing

A novated lease is a three-way arrangement between you, your employer, and a finance company. Your employer leases a car on your behalf and deducts lease payments and running costs from your pre-tax salary. The FBT is calculated on the car's benefit, but because you pay that FBT through pre-tax salary as well, the effective cost is lower than buying the same car outright.

Novated leases suit people who:
- Have a medium-to-high marginal tax rate
- Drive a reasonable number of kilometres (FBT is calculated on an assumed taxable value based on cost and distance)
- Want to roll vehicle running costs into a predictable monthly amount

Electric vehicles attract a full FBT exemption from April 2025, making them particularly attractive to package.

## Things to Watch

- **HECS repayments:** Salary packaging can reduce your taxable income but may not reduce your HELP repayment income (which includes reportable fringe benefits). Check whether your packaging arrangement creates a reportable fringe benefit amount.
- **Medicare Levy Surcharge:** If packaging pushes your income below the MLS threshold ($93,000 for singles), you may be able to avoid the surcharge — but only if you hold qualifying private hospital cover anyway.
- **Government co-contributions and low-income super tax offset:** These have income tests, so reducing your taxable income through packaging may help you qualify.
- **Loan applications:** Some lenders use taxable income for serviceability. Salary packaging reduces taxable income, which can hurt your borrowing capacity.

## Is Salary Packaging Right for You?

The higher your marginal tax rate, the more you save. At 30% and above, packaging FBT-exempt items is almost always worthwhile.
