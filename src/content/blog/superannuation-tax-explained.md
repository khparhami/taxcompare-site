Superannuation is one of the most tax-effective structures in Australia — contributions, earnings, and withdrawals are all taxed at concessional rates compared to ordinary income. But the rules at each stage are different, and understanding them helps you make the most of your retirement savings.

## The Three Stages of Super Tax

### Stage 1: Contributions

There are two types of contributions, each taxed differently.

**Concessional contributions (pre-tax)**

These are contributions made from pre-tax income:
- **Employer SGC contributions** (12% of salary in 2025–26)
- **Salary sacrifice** contributions
- **Personal deductible contributions** (if you're self-employed or choose to claim a deduction)

Tax treatment: **15%** contributions tax is charged inside the fund. For most workers this is far below their marginal income tax rate, making the super fund a highly tax-efficient place to grow money.

The **concessional contributions cap** is $30,000 per year (including SGC). Exceeding it means the excess is included in your personal tax return and taxed at your marginal rate, minus a 15% offset to avoid double taxation.

**Non-concessional contributions (after-tax)**

These are personal contributions made from your after-tax pay — money on which you've already paid income tax. No additional contributions tax applies inside the fund.

The **non-concessional cap** is $120,000 per year. You can bring forward up to three years ($360,000) if your total super balance is below $1.9 million.

**Division 293 Tax — High Earners**

If your income plus concessional super contributions exceed **$250,000**, an additional 15% "Division 293" tax applies to the concessional contributions (bringing the effective rate to 30%). This reduces the super advantage for very high earners, though 30% is still lower than the 45% marginal rate.

### Stage 2: Earnings Inside the Fund

While your money sits in a super fund during the **accumulation phase**, earnings are taxed at a maximum rate of **15%**. Capital gains on assets held longer than 12 months receive a one-third discount, effectively reducing the CGT rate to 10%.

This compares favourably to investing the same money in your own name, where earnings are taxed at your full marginal rate (up to 45%).

Once you enter the **retirement phase** (a pension account), earnings on assets supporting an income stream up to the **$1.9 million transfer balance cap** are tax-free — 0%.

### Stage 3: Withdrawals

Tax on withdrawals depends on your age and how the money is held.

**Age 60 and over — virtually all withdrawals are tax-free**

Once you reach 60 and meet a condition of release, both lump sums and pension income from a taxed super fund are completely tax-free. This is the reward for decades of concessional treatment on the way in.

**Age 55–59 (preservation age)**

Withdrawals are taxed at your marginal rate, but you receive a **15% tax offset** on the taxable component. The tax-free component (from non-concessional contributions) is always withdrawn tax-free.

**Below preservation age — generally inaccessible**

Super is preserved until you reach preservation age (currently 60) and retire, or meet another condition of release (permanent incapacity, terminal illness, severe financial hardship in limited circumstances).

## The Superannuation Guarantee (SGC)

Your employer is required to contribute a minimum percentage of your **ordinary time earnings** to your nominated super fund. The current schedule:

| Year | SGC Rate |
|---|---|
| 2023–24 | 11.0% |
| 2024–25 | 11.5% |
| 2025–26 | 12.0% |
| 2026–27 | 12.0% (legislated to remain) |

SGC is paid **on top of** your base salary — it does not come out of your take-home pay. If your employment contract says your salary is "$85,000 plus super," the super is additional. If it says "$85,000 including super," the base salary is $85,000 ÷ 1.12 = $75,893.

## The Low Income Super Tax Offset (LISTO)

Lower-income earners receive a government **LISTO** payment of up to $500 per year into their super fund. It effectively refunds the 15% contributions tax for those earning under $37,000, since their marginal rate (0%–16%) is at or below 15%.

## Lost and Unclaimed Super

Billions of dollars sit in lost or unclaimed super accounts across Australia. If you've changed jobs, moved, or used a default fund, you may have multiple accounts with duplicate fees eating into your balance. Search for lost super via **myGov** → ATO → Super, and consider consolidating into one fund.
