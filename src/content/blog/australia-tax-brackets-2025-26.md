Australia's income tax system is **progressive** — the more you earn, the higher the rate on each extra dollar. But that doesn't mean your entire income is taxed at your top rate. Understanding how the brackets work can save you from a very common (and costly) misconception.

## The 2025–26 Tax Brackets

For Australian residents, the tax rates for the 2025–26 financial year are:

| Taxable Income | Tax on This Portion |
|---|---|
| $0 – $18,200 | Nil |
| $18,201 – $45,000 | 16c for each $1 over $18,200 |
| $45,001 – $135,000 | $4,288 + 30c for each $1 over $45,000 |
| $135,001 – $190,000 | $31,288 + 37c for each $1 over $135,000 |
| $190,001 and over | $51,638 + 45c for each $1 over $190,000 |

These are the same brackets introduced with the Stage 3 tax cuts in 2024–25 and carry forward into 2025–26.

## What Changed With Stage 3?

Before the Stage 3 cuts (in 2023–24), the 32.5% rate applied from $45,001 to $120,000. Stage 3 replaced this with a flatter 30% rate that stretches all the way to $135,000. The effect:

- **Lower earners ($45k–$135k):** Pay 30% instead of 32.5% on that band — a saving of up to $1,875.
- **Higher earners ($120k–$135k):** Also benefit from the lower 30% rate on money previously taxed at 37%.

## Marginal Rate vs Effective Rate

This is where most people get confused.

**Marginal rate** is the rate that applies to your *next dollar* of income. If you earn $90,000, your marginal rate is 30% — but you haven't paid 30% on everything.

**Effective rate** is the actual percentage of your *total* income paid in tax. On $90,000 in 2025–26:

- $0–$18,200: $0 tax
- $18,201–$45,000: $4,288
- $45,001–$90,000: $13,500

Total tax before offsets: **$17,788**. After the Low Income Tax Offset (LITO) of $0 at this income level, plus 2% Medicare levy ($1,800), total deductions are about **$19,588**.

That's an effective rate of roughly **21.8%** — not 30%.

## The Low Income Tax Offset (LITO)

Resident taxpayers earning up to $66,667 receive a LITO that reduces their tax payable:

- **Up to $37,500:** Full offset of $700
- **$37,501–$45,000:** Offset phases out at 5c per dollar
- **$45,001–$66,667:** Offset phases out at 1.5c per dollar

This means someone earning $37,500 pays effectively **zero** income tax (before Medicare).

## Medicare Levy

Most residents pay a **2% Medicare levy** on top of their income tax. The levy phases in from the low-income threshold ($26,000 in 2025–26) to avoid a sharp cliff.

High earners without private hospital cover may also pay the **Medicare Levy Surcharge** (1%–1.5%), which is separate and not included in the base calculator.

## Non-Residents Pay More

Foreign residents for tax purposes don't get the $18,200 tax-free threshold and face a flat 30% rate on the first $135,000:

| Taxable Income | Rate |
|---|---|
| $0 – $135,000 | 30% |
| $135,001 – $190,000 | 37% |
| $190,001 and over | 45% |

There's no LITO and no Medicare levy for non-residents.
