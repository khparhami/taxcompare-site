Income tax is the largest single deduction from most Australian pay cheques, yet many people aren't sure exactly how it's calculated. This guide covers everything from who must pay, to how the brackets stack, to what actually lands in your bank account.

## Who Pays Australian Income Tax?

Anyone who earns taxable income in Australia is generally required to lodge a tax return with the Australian Taxation Office (ATO). This includes:

- **Employees** receiving wages or salary
- **Self-employed** individuals and sole traders
- **Investors** earning dividends, rent, or capital gains
- **Foreign residents** earning Australian-sourced income

The ATO collects most tax through the **Pay As You Go (PAYG) withholding** system, where your employer deducts the estimated tax from every pay and remits it on your behalf. Your annual tax return reconciles what was withheld against what you actually owe.

## The Tax-Free Threshold

Australian residents don't pay income tax on the first **$18,200** they earn each year. This is called the tax-free threshold. You claim it by ticking the box on your Tax File Number (TFN) declaration when you start a new job.

If you don't claim it — or if you're a foreign resident — tax is deducted from your very first dollar of income.

## How the Progressive Rate System Works

Australia's income tax is **progressive**: each bracket's rate only applies to the slice of income that falls within it, never to your total earnings.

For 2025–26, the resident brackets are:

| Taxable Income | Rate |
|---|---|
| $0 – $18,200 | 0% |
| $18,201 – $45,000 | 16% |
| $45,001 – $135,000 | 30% |
| $135,001 – $190,000 | 37% |
| $190,001 and above | 45% |

**Example — $75,000 salary in 2025–26:**

| Bracket | Calculation | Tax |
|---|---|---|
| $0 – $18,200 | 0% | $0 |
| $18,201 – $45,000 | $26,800 × 16% | $4,288 |
| $45,001 – $75,000 | $30,000 × 30% | $9,000 |
| **Total bracket tax** | | **$13,288** |

After applying the Low Income Tax Offset (LITO) of $0 at this income level, then adding the 2% Medicare levy ($1,500), the total deductions are about **$14,788** — an effective rate of **19.7%**, not 30%.

## Taxable Income vs Gross Income

The tax is calculated on your **taxable income**, which is your gross income minus any allowable deductions. Common deductions include:

- Work-related expenses (equipment, uniforms, home office)
- Self-education costs directly related to your current work
- Charitable donations to deductible gift recipients (DGRs)
- Tax agent fees

This means someone earning $80,000 who claims $3,000 in legitimate deductions only pays tax on $77,000.

## The Low Income Tax Offset (LITO)

The LITO is a non-refundable offset that reduces the tax payable for lower earners:

- Full offset of **$700** for incomes up to $37,500
- Phases out between $37,500 and $66,667

Someone on $37,500 receives the full $700 LITO — combined with the tax-free threshold, they pay **zero** income tax (Medicare still applies).

## Medicare Levy

On top of income tax, most residents pay a **2% Medicare levy**. It funds Australia's public health system. The levy phases in gradually above a low-income threshold ($26,000 in 2025–26) to prevent a sudden jump for low-income earners.

High earners without private hospital cover may also owe the **Medicare Levy Surcharge** (MLS) of 1%–1.5%, which is separate.

## What Your Employer Deducts

Each pay period, your employer uses ATO tax withheld tables to estimate your annual liability and deducts a proportional amount. At year end your return reconciles this:

- **Refund:** You were over-withheld (common if you have deductions or weren't employed all year)
- **Tax bill:** You were under-withheld (common with multiple jobs or investment income)

## Putting It All Together

For a $75,000 salary in 2025–26 (resident, no HELP debt):

| Component | Amount |
|---|---|
| Gross income | $75,000 |
| Income tax (after LITO) | $13,288 |
| Medicare levy (2%) | $1,500 |
| **Total deductions** | **$14,788** |
| **Net take-home (annual)** | **$60,212** |
| Effective tax rate | 19.7% |
| Marginal tax rate | 30% |
