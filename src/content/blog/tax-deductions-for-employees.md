One of the most effective ways to reduce your tax bill is to claim legitimate work-related deductions. The ATO allows employees to deduct expenses incurred in earning their income — but the rules are specific, and claiming things you're not entitled to is the fastest way to attract an audit.

## The Three Golden Rules

To claim a deduction, an expense must satisfy **all three** of the following:

1. **You paid for it yourself** — not reimbursed by your employer
2. **It was directly related to earning your income** — not private or personal
3. **You have a record to prove it** — receipt, bank statement, or other documentation

If an expense is partly work-related and partly personal, you can only claim the work-related portion.

## What You Can Claim

### Vehicle and Travel

- **Car expenses** for travel between two separate workplaces, or from home to an alternative workplace (not your regular commute)
- **Work-related travel** to client meetings, conferences, or sites not at your regular workplace
- **Overnight travel** including accommodation, meals, and incidental expenses where travel is required for work

You **cannot** claim the home-to-work commute. It's private travel regardless of how far you live from work.

Two methods for car claims:
- **Cents per kilometre** (88 cents/km in 2024–25; subject to update) — up to 5,000 km, no log book required but you must be able to justify the distance
- **Log book method** — calculate business-use percentage over a 12-week representative period; claim that percentage of all car running costs

### Clothing and Laundry

- **Protective clothing** — safety boots, hi-vis gear, hard hats
- **Occupation-specific clothing** — a nurse's uniform, chef's whites, tradesperson's branded workwear
- **Compulsory uniforms** with an employer logo (registered with AusIndustry)

You **cannot** claim conventional clothing — suits, business shirts, and office attire are not deductible even if your employer expects you to wear them.

Laundry: $1 per load (work clothing only) or actual cost. No receipts needed under $150/year total laundry and dry cleaning.

### Self-Education

Deductible when the study:
- Maintains or improves specific skills for your **current** job
- Is likely to result in increased income from your **current** employment

You **cannot** claim study costs for a new career or a job you don't yet have, even if the course is related to your industry.

Deductible education costs include:
- Course fees (not HECS — that's a separate system)
- Textbooks, stationery, and equipment
- Travel to attend the course
- Internet portion for study

A $250 reduction applies to education deductions that include tuition fees.

### Home Office

As covered separately, the ATO's fixed rate is **67 cents per hour** for 2025–26 (covering electricity, internet, phone, and consumables), plus separate claims for equipment depreciation. A full-year hours log is required — see the dedicated [work from home deductions article](/blog/work-from-home-tax-deductions/).

### Tools, Equipment, and Technology

- Work tools and equipment you own and use for work
- Work-related software and subscriptions
- Mobile phone (work-related percentage)
- Laptop or computer (work-related percentage)

Items costing **$300 or less** with predominantly work-related use can be claimed immediately. More expensive items must be depreciated over their effective life.

### Professional Development and Memberships

- Professional association memberships and union fees
- Licences and registrations required for your work
- Trade and professional journals, subscriptions to work-relevant publications

### Other Common Deductions

- **Income protection insurance** premiums (not paid through super)
- **Tax agent fees** from last year's return
- **Work-related phone calls** — you need a log for at least one month to establish a business-use percentage
- **Donations** to deductible gift recipients (DGRs) — must be $2 or more

## The $300 Rule — No Receipts Needed

For work-related deductions totalling **$300 or less in total** (excluding car, travel, and meal allowances), you don't need receipts. But you still need to have actually incurred the expenses and be able to explain what they were.

This doesn't mean you can claim $300 without any work-related expenses — it simply removes the record-keeping burden for small genuine claims.

## What You Cannot Claim

- Normal home-to-work travel
- Fines and penalties
- Personal grooming, hair cuts (even if required for work appearance)
- Conventional office clothing
- Childcare costs
- Personal entertainment
- Expenses reimbursed by your employer

## Occupation-Specific Guides

The ATO publishes specific guidance for dozens of occupations including teachers, nurses, police, retail workers, truck drivers, and more. These occupation-specific guides clarify exactly what workers in your field can typically claim. Search "ATO occupation specific deductions" for your role.

## Maximising Your Deductions

The more you earn, the more each deduction saves. At a 30% marginal rate, every $1,000 in legitimate deductions saves $300 in tax. At 37%, it's $370 — and at 45%, $450.
